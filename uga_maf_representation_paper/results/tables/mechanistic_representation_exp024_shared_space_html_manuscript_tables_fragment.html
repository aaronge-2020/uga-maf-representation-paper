Table 1. Shared-space simulation design

ElementSpecification
QuestionCan UGA provide useful cross-modality evidence even when SBS, DBS, and indel exposures are solved separately?
Synthetic cohort520 tumors with sparse mixtures of 12 latent mutational processes.
Mutation modalitiesSBS96, DBS78, and ID83 standard channel universes plus event-level 48D UGA clean-context vectors.
UGA encodingAtlas model compact_event_legacy_d10; layout [L context, REF payload, R context, ALT payload].
Separate solve benchmarkNNLS is fit independently for each modality using either categorical signatures or UGA signature centroids.
Naive pooled benchmarkAll SBS/DBS/ID events are collapsed into a single burden-weighted 48D mean and fit against all modality-specific UGA centroids at once.
Shared-space benchmarkUGA centroids are used for cross-modality nearest-neighbor retrieval and SBS-to-DBS/ID held-out exposure imputation.
Standard-catalog baselineSBS, DBS, and ID channels are unequal axes, so cross-catalog distance is undefined without external labels; chance/tie expectation is reported.
